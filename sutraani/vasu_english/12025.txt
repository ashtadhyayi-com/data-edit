The सेट् क्त्वा is optionally कित् , according to the opinion of ऋषि काश्यप after the verbs तृष् 'to be thirsty', मृष् 'to sprinkle' and कृश् 'to become lean'.
As तृषित्वा or तर्षित्वा 'having thirsted,' मृषित्वा or मर्षित्वा 'having sprinkled,' कृशित्वा or कर्शित्वा 'having become lean.' By <i>sutra</i> [[1.2.18]], the <i>set</i> <i>ktva</i> is never <i>kit</i>. This <i>sutra</i> adds another exception to that general rule.

The special mention of <i>Kasyapa</i> is for the sake of showing respect. The वा is already understood in this <i>sutra</i> by <i>Anuvritti</i> from <i>sutra</i> [[1.2.23]].
