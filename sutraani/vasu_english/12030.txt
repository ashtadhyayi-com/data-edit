The vowel that is perceived as having a low tone is called अनुदात्तः or gravely accented
The word "vowel" of <i>sutra</i> [[1.2.28]] is understood in this <i>sutra</i>. As अग्निः <i>agnih</i>, here अ has the grave accent. The vowel accent known as grave or <i>anudatta</i> is pronounced by lowering the voice. In writing, the <i>anudatta</i> is marked by a line underneath the vowel. The vowel which is uttered from the lower portions of its special place of pronunciation gets the grave accent.
This word is used in <i>sutra</i> [[3.1.4]] \[1\] "the case terminations called सुप् and those affixes that have an indicatory प् are <i>anudatta</i>" &c. As त्रयः, बोधयामि.

In pronouncing an <i>anudatta</i> vowel, there is slackening of the organs, and mildness, as well as softness and sweetness of tone: while there is expansion and widening of the larynx.
