Thus तुल्यो देवदत्तेन or देवदत्तस्य, सदृशो देवदत्तेन, or देवदत्तस्य  ॥ But with तुला and उपमा the Genitive must necessarily be employed. As तुला देवदत्तस्य नास्ति, उपमा कृष्णस्य न विद्यते ॥

Though the <i>anuvritti</i> of वा was understood in this <i>sutra</i> from the previous <i>sutra</i>, yet the repetition of अन्यतरस्याम् is for the sake of the subsequent <i>sutra</i>. The word च in [[2.3.73]] attracts the word अन्यतरस्याम् into that <i>sutra</i>; but had the word अन्यतरस्याम् not been used in this <i>sutra</i>, then the word च would have attracted the word तृतीया instead, which is not desired.

On this <i>sutra</i>, Professor <i>Apte</i> says:-- '<i>Panini</i> says that the words तुला and उपमा can not be used with the Instrumental. But this is against good usage : as तुलां, यदारोहति दंतवाससा (<i>Kum</i>. <i>Sam</i>. V. 24), नभसा तुलां समाररोह (<i>Raghuvamsa</i> VIII. 15). स्फुठोपमं भूतिसितेन शंभुना (<i>Magh</i> I. 4).'
