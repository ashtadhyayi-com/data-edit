Thus, प्लक्षन्यग्रोधम् or प्लक्षन्यग्रोधाः 'the <i>Plaksha</i> and the <i>Nyagrodha</i> trees'; रुरुपृषतम् or <i>रुरुपृषताः 'the </i>Ruru<i>-deer and the spotted antelopes', कुशकाशम् or कुशकाशाः 'the </i>Kusa<i> grass and the </i>Kasa<i> grass'; व्रीहियवम् or व्रीहियवाः 'the rice and the barley' दधिघृतम् or ते 'the curd and the butter' गोमहिषम् or षाः 'the cow and the buffalo'; तित्तिरकपिञ्जलम् or लाः 'the </i>Tittiri<i> (a kind of partridge) and the </i>Kapinjala<i> birds'; अश्ववडवम् or वाः 'the horse and the mare'; पूर्वापरम् or रे 'the first and the last'; अधरोत्तरम् or रे 'the upper and lower.'

Vart:- The </i>Dvandva<i> compound of words denoting names of fruits, army, large trees, wild animals, birds, small animals grain and grass, is singular, when a large number or quantity of these is spoken of; that is when a large number of them is taken collectively, the compound is singular, otherwise not; as, बदरामलके तिष्ठतः 'a </i>badari<i> and an </i>amalaki<i> fruit are here'; रथिकाश्वारोहौ 'a charioteer and a horseman'; प्लक्षन्यग्रोधौ 'a </i>Plaksha<i> and a </i>Nyagrodha<i> tree'; रुरुपृषतौ 'a </i>Ruru<i> and a </i>Prishata<i> deer'; हंसचक्रवाकौ 'a </i>Hansa<i> and a </i>Chakravaka<i> bird'; यूकालिक्षे 'a louse and nit', व्रीहियवौ 'a </i>brihi<i> and </i>yava<i> grain'; कुशकाशौ 'the </i>kusa<i> and </i>kasa_ grass.'
