Thus अत्र 'here,' and अतस् 'hence' in the following :- एतस्मिन् ग्रामे सुखं वसामः ; अथो अत्र युक्ता अधीमहे । 'We live in this (<i>etasmin</i>) village, happily, therefore let us read with full devotion in this (<i>atra</i>).' एतस्माच्छात्राच्छन्दोऽधीष्व, अथो अतो व्याकरणमप्यधीष्व । 'Learn from this student Prosody and also learn from him Grammar.' By rule (V. 3. 5.) अश् is declared to be the substitute of एतद्; its repetition here is to indicate that this अश् of <i>anvadesa</i> is <i>anudatta</i>.
