Where a word is governed by a कर्मप्रवचनीयाः [[1.4.83]] in the sense of 'more than' उपोऽधिके च [[1.4.87]] or 'lord of' अधिरीश्वरे [[1.4.97]] there the 7th case-affix (locative) is used.
Ex. उपखार्याम् द्रोणः 'A <i>Drona</i> is more than a <i>Khari</i>,' अधिब्रह्मदत्ते पंचालाः '<i>Brahmadatta</i> is the lord of <i>Panchalas</i>.' The phrase यस्यचेश्वरवचनं indicate that both the thing owned and the owner may be in the locative. See (1.4.97).

This aphorism debars the accusative.
