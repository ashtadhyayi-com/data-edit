A द्वन्द compound of words denoting क्रतु (sacrifices) ordained by यजुर् (अध्वर्यु) Veda, is singular, provided they are never used in the neuter gender
As, अर्काश्वमेधम् the <i>Arks</i> and <i>Asvamedha</i> sacrifices,' सायाह्नातिरात्रम् 'the <i>Sayahna</i> and <i>Atiratra</i> sacrifices.

Why do we say '<i>Yajur</i>-<i>veda</i> sacrifices' ? Observe इषुवज्रौ 'the <i>lshu</i> and <i>Vajra</i> ceremonies,' उद्भिद्वलभिदौ ॥

Why do we say 'when not in the neuter gender' ? Observe राजसूयवाजपेयौ 'the <i>Rajasuya</i> and <i>Vajapeya</i> sacrifices.'

Why have we दर्शपौर्णमासौ ? Because the word क्रतुः is a technical term applying only to <i>Soma</i>-sacrifices and not any sacrifices in general. Therefore, though <i>Darsa</i> and <i>Paurnamasa</i> are <i>Yajur</i>-<i>veda</i> ceremonies, they not being <i>Soma</i> ceremonies, the present rule, does not apply.
