In the मन्त्र literature,the affix णिवन् comes after the verb यज् 'to sacrifice' when in composition the word अव्।
As अवयाज्, nom. sing. अवयाः (<i>Rig</i>. <i>Ved</i>. I. 173. 12) 'name of a Vedic priest'; as त्वं यज्ञे वरुणस्यावया असि 'Thou art the priest of <i>Varuna</i> in the sacrifice'.

The division of this aphorism from the last in which it could have been included, is for the sake of the subsequent <i>sutras</i> in which the <i>anuvritti</i> of यज only runs. This word is thus declined:-

- 1st. अवयाः, अवयाजौ, अवयाजः
- 2nd. अवयाजं, अवयाजौ, अवयाजः  
- 3rd. अवयाजा, अवयोभ्यां, अवयोभिः
