The affix श्यन् comes after a root of the दिवादि class when a सार्वधातुक affix denoting the agent follows.
This debars शप्. The servile न of श्यन् is for the sake of accent [[6.1.197]], showing that the <i>udatta</i> accent falls on the radical verb and not on the affix; and the indicatory श makes the affix <i>sarvadhatuka</i>; as दिव् + श्यन् + तिप् = दीव् + य + ति [[7.2.77]] = दीव्यति; so also सीव्यति. The <i>Divadi</i> verbs belong to the Fourth conjugation.
