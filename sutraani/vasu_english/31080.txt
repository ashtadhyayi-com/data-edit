The affix उ comes after the roots धिन्व 'to please' and कृणिव 'to hurt', when a सार्वधातुक affix denoting the agent follows and in these verbs अ is the final substitute of the final इ।
The root धिन्व or धिन्वि belongs to the <i>Bhuadi</i> class. Thus धिन्व + उ + ति = धिन् + अ + उ + ति = धिन् + उ + ति [[6.4.48]] = धिनोति 'he pleases'. It might be asked, why there is no <i>guna</i> of the इ of धिन् by rule [[7.3.86]] which requires the base to be <i>gunated</i> before a <i>sarvadhatuka</i> or an <i>ardhadhatuka</i> affix. The answer is to be found in <i>sutra</i> [[1.1.57]]; the substitute of अ which is a blank, is like the former occupant (<i>sthanivat</i>), and thus prevents <i>guna</i>.
