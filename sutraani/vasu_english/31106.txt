After the root वद् 'to speak', governing a case-inflected word as its उपपद and not having a preposition annexed to it, there comes the affix क्यप् also.
The phrase 'not having a preposition' is to be read into this <i>sutra</i> by <i>anuvritti</i> from <i>sutra</i>100 <i>ante</i>. The force of the word 'also' is to indicate that the affix यत् is also employed. The word सुपि means 'in construction with an <i>upapada</i> that ends in case-affix'.

Thus ब्रह्म + वद् + क्यप् = ब्रह्म + वद् + य = ब्रह्मोद्य; ब्रह्म + वद् + यत् = ब्रह्मवद्यम् 'told by the <i>Vedas</i> or <i>Brahman</i>'. Similarly सत्योद्यम् or सत्यवद्यम् 'truth-mouthed'.

Why do we say 'when it has a case-inflected word as <i>upapada</i>'? Otherwise वद् + ण्यत् = वाद्यम् 'a musical instrument'.

Why do we say 'not having a preposition'? Observe प्रवाद्यम् ' a rumour' formed by the affix ण्यत् and not by यत् or क्यप्.
