And when the words नाडी 'a tube' and मुष्टि 'a fist' are objects in composition with the verbs ध्मा 'to blow' and धे 'to suck', the affix खश् is used.
Here also the irregularly-formed <i>Dvandva</i> compound नाडीमुष्टि showes the non-applicability of the rule of <i>yathasankhya</i> ; for मुष्टि being a घि ought to have stood first [[2.2.32]].

Thus नाडिन्धमः 'blowing through the tube'; मुष्टिन्धमः 'blowing through the fist'; नाडिन्धयः 'sucking through the tube', an infant; मुष्टिन्धयः 'an infant'.

The word 'and' in the aphorism indicates that there are other words also so formed. Thus घटिन्धमः, घटिन्धयः, खारिन्धमः, खारिन्धयः, वातन्धमः, वातन्धयः, 'a mountain'.

This <i>sutra</i> is not <i>Panini</i>'s, but is really a <i>Vartika</i>, raised to the rank of a <i>sutra</i> by the author of the <i>Kasika</i>. The <i>Vartikakara</i> divides the last <i>sutra</i> in this way: (1) स्तने धेटः ॥ (2) ततो मुष्टो ध्मश्च ॥ (3) नासिकायां ध्मश्च धेटश्च ॥ (4) नाडी मुष्टि घटोखारीष्विति वक्तव्यम् ॥
