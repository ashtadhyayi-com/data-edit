The affix लिङ् (Benedictive) comes in all tenses when the words यच्च and यत्र are in composition with it, provided that censure is implied.
The governing force of the words <i>anavaklipti</i> and <i>amarsha</i> ceases. This debars all other tense-affixes. Thus यच्च or यत्र तत्र भवान् वृषलं याजयेत् ॠद्धौ वृद्धः सन् ब्राह्मणः, गर्हामहे, अहो अन्याय्यमेतत् 'we blame you who being an opulent, old <i>Brahmana</i> (who ought to know better) officiate as a sacrificial priest for a <i>Sudra</i>, O! It is improper'!

When the non-completion of the action is indicated, the Conditional tense must be employed under the rules given in [[3.3.139]]- [[3.3.140]]- [[3.3.141]]. As यच्च or यत्र त्वं हरिं निन्देः or अनिन्दिष्यः, गर्हामहे 'we censure that you should (have attempted to) blaspheme <i>Hari</i>', &c.
