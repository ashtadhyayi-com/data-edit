The affix ङीप् is used in the feminine after a प्रातिपदिक (Nominal Stem) ending with the word पाद् when the word denotes a verse of the ऋग्वेद।
This debars ङीप् ॥ Thus द्विपदा ऋक् 'a <i>Rik</i> verse consisting of two quarter verses'; similarly त्रिपदा ऋक्, चतुष्पदा ऋक् ॥ Why do we say "when denoting a ' <i>Rik</i>' verse" ? Observe द्विपदी देवदत्ता '<i>Devadatta</i> (a woman) having two feet'.
