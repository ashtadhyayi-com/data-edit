The affix ङीष् is always used in the छन्दस् (Vedas) in forming the feminine of the words बहु etc.
Thus बह्वीषु हित्वा प्रपिवन् ॥ Here बह्वी is the name of a herb.

The word नित्य 'always' is used in the aphorism, more for the sake of the subsequent <i>sutra</i>, which it governs than for this <i>sutra</i>. For the word 'optionally' does not govern this, and the aphorism even without the word '<i>nitya</i>'? would have been a necessary rule and not optional: for आरम्भसामर्थ्यादेव नित्योविधिः सिद्धः, योगारम्भश्चिन्त्य योजनः ॥
