The affix णिनि comes in the sense of 'enounced by him' after a word in the Instrumental -3rd case in construction when it denotes a ब्राह्मण or a कल्प-work enounced by ancient sages.
This <i>sutra</i> further gives the sense of the affix. पुराण means 'Ancient Sages.' equivalent to पुरातण. Of <i>Brahmana</i> literature, the examples are, शाट्यायनिनः, एतेरायणः ॥ <i>Kalpa</i> work : as पैङ्गीकल्पः, आरुणपराजी ॥

Why do we say पुराणप्रोक्तेषु 'announced by ancient sages.'? Observe याज्ञवल्कानि ब्राह्मणानि, आश्मरथः कल्पः ॥ [[6.4.151]] <i>Yajnavalkya</i> &c being sages of modern times. The तद् विषय affix [[4.2.66]] will not also apply to these, because the word ब्राह्मण in [[4.2.66]] is restricted to पुराणप्रोक्त of the present <i>sutra</i>.
