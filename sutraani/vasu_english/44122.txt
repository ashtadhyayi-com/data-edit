The affix यत् comes in the sense of 'praising' after the words 1. रेवती 2. जगतीand 3. हविष्य in the Genitive -6th-Case in construction, in the छन्दस् (Vedas).
The word प्रशस्य means प्रशंसन 'praising, extolling', formed by adding the <i>Krit</i> affix क्यप् to the root, with the force of भाव or 'condition'. Thus रेवत्यम्॑, जगत्य॑म् and हविष्य॑म् 'praising of <i>Revati</i>, <i>Jagati</i> or <i>Havishya</i>'. The word हविष्यम् is thus formed हविषे हिता = हविष्याः 'things fit for offering' i. e. butter &c, (हविष् + यत् [[5.1.4]]) हविष्यानाम् प्रशंसनं = हविष्यम् (हविष्य + यत् [[4.4.122]]) = हविष्य् + यत् the final अ being elided by [[6.4.148]] = हविष् + यत् the य being elided by [[8.4.64]].
