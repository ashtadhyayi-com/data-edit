The case in construction here is accusative (तद्). Thus, वंशभारं हरति, वहति आवहति वा = वांशभारिकः, कौटजभारिकः, बाल्वजभारिकः ॥

Why do we say "after भार"? Observe वंशं हरति, no affixing. Why do we say "preceded by वंश &c". Observe व्रीहिभारं हरति, no affixing.

According to another explanation, the phrase भाराद् वंशादिभ्यः is explained as भारभूतेभ्यो वंशादिभ्यः. Then the <i>sutra</i> will mean "the above named affixes come after वंश &c. when they denote a load". Thus भारभूतान् वंशान् हरति = वांशिकः; कौटजिकः, बाल्वजिकः ॥ Why do we say "when denoting a load भार"? Observe वंशं हरति ॥ Why do we say after वंश &c.? Observe भारभूतान् व्रीहीन् वहति ॥ Both these explanations are valid and traditional.

The word हरति means to carry to another place or to steal; वहति means to carry on ones back or head, आवहति means to produce or bring.
