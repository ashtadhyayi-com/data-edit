This debars कृत्वसुच् ॥ Thus द्वि-द्विस्, त्रि-त्रिस्, चतुर्-चतुर् in द्विर्भुङ्क्ते, त्रिर्भुङ्क्ते, चतुर्भुङ्क्ते ॥ The स् in the last example is elided by [[8.2.24]]: but चतुर् meaning "four", and चतुर् meaning "four-times", are differently accented : the च् of सुच् indicating that the accent <i>udatta</i> falls on the last syllable. Thus चतुर् (चतेरुरन् <i>Un</i> V. 58, being formed with the affix '<i>uran</i>' added to चत्), and चतुर् (with सुच्).
