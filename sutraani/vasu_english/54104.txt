The affix टच् comes after the word ब्रह्मण in a तत्-पुरुष समास when the compound denotes a person belonging to a certain country.
The word जानपदः = जनपदेषु भवः 'belonging to a country'. This affix comes after that <i>Tatpurusha</i> the first member of which is a word denoting a country. Thus सुराष्ट्रेषु ब्रह्मा = सुराष्ट्रब्रह्मः, and अवन्तिब्रह्मः "a <i>Brahmana</i> of <i>Surashtra</i> or <i>Avanti</i>". This is an example of Locative <i>Tatpurusha</i> compound.

Why do we say "when referring to the name of a country"? Observe देवब्रह्मा नारदः '<i>Narada</i> the bard of the gods'.
