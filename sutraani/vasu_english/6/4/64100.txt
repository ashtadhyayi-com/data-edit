Thus सग्धिश्चमे सपीतिश्च मे, बब्धांते हरी धानाः ॥ सग्धिः is thus derived : क्तिन् is added to the root अद्; then by [[2.4.39]], घस् is substituted for अद्; thus घस् + ति = घस + ति  (अ being elided by the present <i>sutra</i>) = घ् + ति (स् being elided by [[8.2.26]]), then त is changed to ध, and घ to ग and we have ग्धिः ॥ Then समाना ग्धिः = सग्धिः (समान changed to स [[6.3.84]]). The word बब्धाम् is the Imperative of भस्, thus भस् + श्लु + ताम् = भ भस् + ताम् स् = ब भ्स् + ताम् [[6.4.100]] = बभ् + ताम् [[8.2.26]] = ब ब् + धाम् बब्धाम् ॥ This rule of elision being a <i>nitya</i> and a subsequent rule ought to have operated first, but, as a Vedic anomaly, the reduplication takes place first. See [[7.2.67]]

Why do we read "before an affix beginning also with a consonant"? Because the elision takes place before a vowel affix also, as बप्सति = भस् + श्लु + झि = ब भस् + अति (VII. I. 4) बप्सति [[8.4.55]].

Why having an indicatory क् or ङ्? Observe अंशून् बभस्ति ॥ The च has been added into the aphorism by the <i>Vartikakara</i>.
