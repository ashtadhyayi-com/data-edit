There is the substitution of this stem शीर्षन् for शिरस् when a तद्धित-affix beginning with य follows.
The word शीर्षन् is understood here. This rule teaches substitution. The original for which this substitution comes is not given in the <i>sutra</i>, we must infer it. The appropriate original is शिरस् ॥ Thus शीर्षण्यो हि मुख्यो भवति, शीर्षण्यः स्वरः ॥ The affix यत् is here added by [[4.3.55]]. The word शीर्षण् retains its original form before this affix यत्, the final अन् not being replaced by anything else [[6.4.168]]. Why do we say when 'a <i>Taddhita</i>-affix follows'? Observe शिर इच्छति = शिरस्यति, here य is not a <i>Taddhita</i> affix, and so there is no substitution.

Vart:- The substitution is optional when meaning 'hair'. As शीर्षण्याः केशाः or शिरस्याः केशाः ॥
