Also when the short अ , not being final in a पद , is followed by a गुण letter, then in the room of both the precedent and the subsequent - the single substitute is the form of the subsequent i.e. the गुण।
Thus पच् + शप् + अन्ति = पच + अन्ति = पचन्ति so also यजन्ति ॥ This debars lengthening of [[6.1.101]]. पच + ए = पचे, यज + ए = यजे, here the <i>Vriddhi</i> is debarred [[6.1.88]]. Why do we say "after the short vowel अ?" Observe या + अन्ति = यान्ति, वान्ति ॥ Why do we say when followed by 'a <i>Guna</i> letter ?' Observe अपच + इ = अपचे, अयजे ॥ When final in a <i>Pada</i>, this rule does not apply : as दण्ड + अग्रं = दण्डाग्रम्, यूपाग्रम्, क्षुद्राग्रम् ॥
