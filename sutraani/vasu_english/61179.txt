The case-endings beginning with a consonant, have the acute accent after the Numerals called षट् [[1.1.24]], as well as after त्रि and चतुर् ।
The <i>anuvritti</i> of अन्तोदात्ताद् ceases, for the present rule applies even to words like प꣡चन्  and न꣡वन् which are acute on the initial by <i>Phit</i> II.5. Thus षण्णा꣡म्, षड्भिः꣡, षड्भ्यः꣡, पंचाना꣡म्, सप्ताना꣡म्, त्रिभिः꣡, त्रिभ्यः꣡, त्रयाणा꣡म्, चतुर्णा꣡म्  (See VII.1.55). Why do we say 'before case-affixes beginning with consonants'? Observe च꣡तस्रः पश्य [[6.1.167]] and [[7.2.99]].
