The first member of a तत्-पुरुष समास preserves its original accent when the second member is a द्विगु समास (Numeral Determinative Compound) denoting 'measure or quantity'.
Thus प्रा꣡च्यसप्तसमः and गा꣡न्धारिसप्तसमः ॥ The word सप्तसमः = सप्तसमाः प्रमाणमस्य, the affix मात्रच् denoting 'measure' [[5.2.37]] is elided (See [[5.2.37]] <i>Vartika</i>) प्राच्यश्चासौ सप्तमश्च = प्रा꣡च्यसप्तसमः "an Eastern seven-years old". So also गान्धा꣡रिसप्तसमः or षट्समः ॥ प्राच्य has acute on the first; while गान्धारि is either accented on the first or on the middle; as it belongs to the <i>Kardamadi</i> class (<i>Phit</i> III.10). Why do we say 'before a <i>Dvigu</i>'? Observe व्रीहिप्रस्थः ॥ Why do we say 'when denoting measure?' Observe परमसप्तसमः ॥
