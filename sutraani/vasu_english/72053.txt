The affixes क्त्वा , क्त and क्त्वातु get the इट् augment after the root अञ्च् when meaning 'to honour or do something to show honour'.
As अञ्चित्वा जानु जुहोति, अञ्चिता अस्य गुरवः [[3.2.188]], [[6.4.30]], [[2.3.67]] By [[7.2.56]] अञ्चु would optionally have caused इट् to come before <i>ktva</i>, and hence by [[7.2.15]] never before the <i>Nishtha</i>. This <i>sutra</i> ordains invariable addition of this augment. Why do we say when meaning 'to honor'? Observe उदक्तमुदकं कूपात्, 'the water is raised from the well'.
