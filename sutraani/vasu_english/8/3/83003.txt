Thus [[8.3.9]] teaches रु substitution of म, the long आ preceding it, would have been optionally nasal by the last <i>sutra</i>. The present <i>sutra</i> makes it necessarily so. Thus महाँ असि (<i>Rig</i>. III. 46. 2) महाँ इन्द्रो य ओजसा (<i>Rig</i>. VIII. 6. I). देवाँ अच्छादीव्यत् ॥

Some (i. e. the <i>Taittariyas</i>) read it as <i>anusvara</i>. This is a Vedic diversity.

Why do we say "for a long आ"? Observe ये वा वनस्पतीरनु ॥ Why do we say "when a vowel or ह, य, व, or र follows"? Observe भवांश्चरति, भवांश्छादयति ॥

The word नित्य 'always' is employed for the sake of distinctness only. The very fact of making a separate <i>sutra</i>, would give it a compulsory force, even without the word <i>nitya</i>.
