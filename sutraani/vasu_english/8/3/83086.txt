As अभिनिष्टानो वर्णः, अभिनिष्टानो विसर्जनीयः or अभिनिस्तानो वर्णः or विसर्जनीयः ॥ The compound preposition अभि-निस् causes this change, and not any one of them separately. Thus <i>Apastamba</i> :- द्व्यक्षरं चतुरक्षरं वा नामपूर्वमाख्यातोत्तरं दीर्घभिनिष्टान्तं, घोषवदाद्यन्तरन्तस्थम् "a name (<i>nama</i>) should be such that it should consist of two-syllables or four-syllables, that its first portion should be a noun (<i>nama</i>-<i>purva</i>), and its second portion a verb (<i>akhyata</i>), that it should end in a long vowel (<i>dirgha</i>) or a <i>visarga</i> (<i>abhinishtana</i>), that the first letter of such a noun should be a <i>ghosha</i> or sonant letter (<i>ghosha</i>-<i>vad</i>-<i>adi</i>), and a semi vowel (<i>antastha</i>) should be in the body of it." Thus the names द्रविणोदा, वरिवोदा fulfill these conditions and are good names.

Why do we say "when it is the name of a particular letter."? Observe अभिनिस्तनति मृदङ्गः ॥ The <i>anuvritti</i> of समासे ceases from this place.
