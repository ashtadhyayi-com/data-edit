Of the two Vocatives of the same form standing at the beginning of a sentence, the end vowel of the first becomes प्लुत and स्वरित when envy, praise, anger or blame is expressed.
The vocative is doubled by [[8.1.8]]. This <i>sutra</i> ordains <i>pluta</i> there. Thus Envy:- माणवका॑ ३ माणवक, अभिरूपका॑३ अभिरूपक, रिक्तं त अभिरूप्यम् ॥ Praise:- माणवका॑३ माणवक, अ꣡भिरूपका॑३ अभिरूपक शोभनः खल्वसि ॥ Anger:- माणवका॑३ माणवक, अ꣡विनीतका॑३ अविनीतक इदानीं ज्ञास्यसि जाल्म ॥ Blame:- शाक्तीका॑३ शाक्तीक, वाष्टीका३ वाष्टीक रिक्ता ते शक्तिः ॥

Vart:- The word "optionally" should be read into this <i>sutra</i>. Thus there is no <i>pluta</i> and <i>svarita</i> in one alternative. As माणवक माणवक &c.
